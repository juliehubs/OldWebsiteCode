Project Description

WHO IS KANGA?

Kanga is a local Atlanta start up focused on delivering "everything from A to Z from A to B".

PROJECT BRIEF

Through General Assembly's UXDi Course, I with my two teammates were tasked on helping Kanga make their app on brand as well as help with creating a better user flow and experience.

MY ROLE

I assisted my team with the technological possibilities, brand identification, annotated wireframes, iconography, prototypes, and app flow.

Kanga is trying to be everything to everyone, but lacks an overall feeling of security and simplicity because everything is so open in the current application.

Research

INTERVIEWING AND DATA

Initial research was compromised of two main parts: a digital survey and a card sort. Our survey was used to get a better understand of a who a user may be, what delivery pain points are, and any similar experiences. We also had a digital card sorting exercise in which we asked our participant to lay out how they would want to work through such an application.
PERSONAS

After completing our research, we then developed 3 personas. One for business users, one who wants to track their order, and one who is in a rush. We concluded our persona development by focusing
on in a pinch Paige.
USER FLOW

From all of our testing and research, including competitive and comparative analysis, we developed a new app flow. Some of the important changes we included were choosing to require credit card information for logging in, and starting the delivery flow with vehicle selection.

Design

ICONS

Icons were made to add a fun feeling to the app as a whole. Most were used within the onboarding tutorial. Icons were created using Pixelmator and Adobe Illustrator. We also incorporated more of Hank, Kanga's mascot.
TECHNOLOGY

Besides the APIs and technology already used within the app, we recomended new APIs, all of which were free, to be added on for a better user experience.
ANNOTATED WIREFRAMES

With all of our designs completed, we were able to deliver high fidelity wireframes with annotations of our changes and recommendations.
